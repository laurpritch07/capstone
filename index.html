I think most designers today are aware of the importance of making design accessible. However, it can be difficult to know where to start. When I started looking for guidance, I was overwhelmed by the amount of information on the internet and even more overwhelmed by the conflicting information I found when talking to other designers and developers on projects. I would present ideas without feeling confident that I understood the accessibility guidance. I craved a more holistic approach to designing accessibly - one based on iteration, testing and refinement. A process that considered accessibility from the beginning. Part visual exploration, part case study, Accessibly serves as a learning and inspiration resource for those working in graphic and web design.
